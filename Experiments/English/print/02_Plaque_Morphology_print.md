# Plaque Morphology

**Date:** 2024-11-24 ~ 2024-11-30 | **Operator:** Yuhang Gong | **Status:** Completed

## Objective
Observe and document plaque morphology characteristics of 5 phage strains (R1, R2, R3, W1, W2) on their respective host bacteria.

## Materials

**Phages:**
| Name | Host | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| R1, R2, R3 | EcAZ-2-OVA | R3: High titer |
| W1, W2 | EcAZ-1 | W2: Isolated from W1 |

**Reagents:** LB agar plates (1.5%), Soft agar (0.7%, 3 mL/plate), Log-phase bacteria (OD₆₀₀ ~0.5, 200 µL/plate)

**Equipment:** 37°C incubator, Ruler/Caliper

## Protocol

**Double-Layer Agar Method:**
1. Prepare LB bottom agar plates
2. Soft agar cool to ~50°C + 200 µL bacteria + diluted phage
3. Pour onto bottom layer; Incubate at 37°C overnight

**Observation:** Measure diameter (n≥5), Record clarity (Clear/Turbid), Note halo presence

## Experimental Records
| Date | Operation | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| 11-24~30 | Morphology observation | Observe during stock titer determination |

## Results

### Raw Measurements
| Phage | Measurements (mm) | Average |
|-------|-------------------|---------|
| R1 | 0.1, 0.1, 0.2, 0.1, 0.2, 0.1 | 0.1–0.2 |
| R2 | 2, 2, 2, 1.5, 1.5, 2 | 1.8±0.3 |
| R3 | 4, 3, 2, 3, 4, 3 | 3.2±0.8 |
| W1 | 0.5, 0.5, 0.5, 0.5, 0.5, 0.5 | ~0.5 |
| W2 | 2, 3, 2, 2, 2, 2 | 2.2±0.4 |

### Morphology Summary
| Phage | Diameter (mm) | Clarity | Halo | Characteristics |
|-------|---------------|---------|------|-----------------|
| R1 | 0.1–0.2 | Clear | Yes | Very small |
| R2 | 1.8±0.3 | **Turbid** | No | Turbid plaques |
| R3 | 3.2±0.8 | Clear | Yes | Largest |
| W1 | ~0.5 | Clear | Yes | Small |
| W2 | 2.2±0.4 | Clear | Yes | Medium |

### Size Classification
- **Very small (<0.5 mm):** R1 (0.1–0.2 mm)
- **Small (0.5–1 mm):** W1 (0.5 mm)
- **Medium (1–2.5 mm):** R2 (1.8 mm), W2 (2.2 mm)
- **Large (>2.5 mm):** R3 (3.2 mm)

## Conclusions
1. The 5 phage strains show distinct differences in plaque size
2. **R2 produces turbid plaques** → possible temperate/lysogenic phage
3. R3 has the largest plaques and highest titer
